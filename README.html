TWLDA (Term Weighting LDA)

TWLDA is a new topic of LDA which assigns low weights to words with low topic discriminating power. For more details, please refer to "Exploring Topic Discriminating Power of Words in Latent Dirichlet
Allocation" created by Kai Yang, Yi Cai and Zhenhong Chen.

TWLDA

Step1: φ′⃗←TopicModel()\vec{\varphi'}\gets TopicModel()φ′​←TopicModel()

In Step 1, a topic model is executed. Then a topic-word distribution φ′⃗\vec{\varphi'}φ′​ is generated by this topic model.

Step2: σ⃗←Calculate(φ′⃗)\vec{\sigma}\gets Calculate(\vec{\varphi'})σ←Calculate(φ′​)

In Step2, according to the φ′⃗\vec{\varphi'}φ′​, we apply a supervised term weighting scheme to calculate weights of word σ⃗\vec{\sigma}σ. Since supervised schemes have the ability to measure the topic discriminating power of words, in principle, all the supervised term weighting schemes can be applied here.

Step3: Discounting the number of words

Step3 is to discount the number of words by their weights. The number of words is diminished proportionally accoding to weights of words. Hence, the total discounted number of words in document mmm under topic kkk is calculated as follow:
n′m(k)=∑t=1Vσtnmktn'{_m^{(k)}}=\sum_{t=1}^V\sigma_tn_{mkt}n′m(k)​=t=1∑V​σt​nmkt​
where σt\sigma_tσt​ denates the weight of word ttt, which is ranging from 0 to 1. nnktn_{nkt}nnkt​ is the number of word ttt belonging to topic kkk in document mmm. Similarly, the total discounted number of word ttt under topic kkk is calculated as follows:
n′k(t)=∑m=1Mσtnmktn'{_k^{(t)}}=\sum_{m=1}^M\sigma_tn_{mkt}n′k(t)​=m=1∑M​σt​nmkt​

Step4: Executing xLDA with the discounted values

Step4 is to excute the standard LDA or its variants, denoted as xLDA, using the discounted calues calculated in Equations 1 and 2.
